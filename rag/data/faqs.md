## General Hospital Information

Q: What is the main address of Saint Jude Hospital?
A: Saint Jude Hospital is located at 123 Care Lane, Medical City, MC 90210. The main entrance is accessible directly from Care Lane, while the emergency entrance is located on the East side of the campus.

Q: What is the main phone number of the hospital?
A: You can reach our main operator by calling (555) 0100. This line is available twenty-four hours a day to assist with general directories or transfers. For emergencies, please dial 911 immediately.

Q: What are the general visiting hours at Saint Jude Hospital?
A: General visiting hours are daily from 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM. Visitors are asked to check in at the front desk upon arrival to receive a guest pass. Children under twelve must be accompanied by an adult.

Q: What are the visiting hours for special care units like the ICU?
A: The Intensive Care Unit and Neonatal Intensive Care Unit have restricted visiting hours from 10:00 AM to 12:00 PM and from 5:00 PM to 7:00 PM daily. Only immediate family members are permitted in these units.

Q: Where should patients and visitors park?
A: Visitor parking is available in Garage A directly opposite the main hospital entrance. Outpatients may also park in Lot B, which is situated next to the outpatient clinic building.

Q: How much does parking cost at the hospital?
A: Parking is free for the first thirty minutes. After that, it costs two dollars per hour up to a daily maximum of ten dollars. Patients receiving long-term treatment can request parking validation.

Q: Is valet parking available?
A: Valet parking is offered at the main entrance and the oncology center from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM, Monday through Friday. There is a flat fee of twelve dollars for valet service.

Q: Where can I find the hospital cafeteria, and what are its hours?
A: The cafeteria is on the ground floor of the main building. It serves breakfast from 7:00 AM to 10:00 AM, lunch from 11:30 AM to 2:00 PM, and dinner from 5:00 PM to 7:00 PM daily.

Q: Are there gift shops or flower shops on site?
A: The Saint Jude Gift Shop is in the main lobby on the first floor. It is open Monday through Friday from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, and Saturdays from 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM. It is closed on Sundays.

Q: Is guest Wi-Fi available at the hospital?
A: Free guest Wi-Fi is available throughout the hospital campus. To connect, select the network labeled "StJude-Guest" on your device and accept the terms of service. No password is required.


## Appointments & Scheduling

Q: How can I schedule an appointment with a doctor?
A: You can schedule an appointment by calling our central scheduling office at (555) 0101. Alternatively, you can book appointments online twenty-four seven through the MyStJude patient portal.

Q: How do I reschedule or cancel an existing appointment?
A: To reschedule or cancel, please call your clinic directly or use the scheduling office at (555) 0101. You can also make these changes online via the MyStJude patient portal up to twenty-four hours before your visit.

Q: What is the cancellation policy for appointments?
A: We require at least twenty-four hours notice for cancellation of scheduled appointments. Failure to cancel in advance may result in a fee or affect future scheduling options. We appreciate your cooperation.

Q: What should I bring to my first appointment?
A: Please bring a valid photo ID, your health insurance card, and a list of all current medications. If you have recent medical records or test results from another clinic, please bring those as well.

Q: What is the policy for late arrivals?
A: If you arrive more than fifteen minutes late, your appointment may need to be rescheduled to ensure other patients are seen on time. Please call ahead if you anticipate being late to your visit.

Q: Do I need a referral to schedule an appointment with a specialist?
A: Referral requirements depend on your insurance provider and the specific department. Please contact your primary care doctor to arrange a referral, or check with your insurance company to see if one is required.

Q: Are telehealth or virtual visits available?
A: Yes, Saint Jude Hospital offers telehealth visits for many primary care and specialty consultations. You can request a virtual visit when scheduling your appointment online or by phone.

Q: How do I prepare for my virtual telehealth visit?
A: Ensure you have a reliable internet connection and a device with a working camera and microphone. You will receive an email link fifteen minutes before your appointment to log in to our secure platform.

Q: What is the pre-registration process before an appointment?
A: Pre-registration can be completed online through the MyStJude portal up to three days before your visit. This allows you to verify your personal details, sign consent forms, and upload insurance cards.

Q: Do I need to fast before laboratory services or a blood draw?
A: Fasting requirements depend on the specific blood tests ordered by your physician. Common tests like lipid panels or glucose tests require fasting for eight to twelve hours. Please check your instructions.

Q: How far in advance should I schedule a routine annual exam?
A: We recommend scheduling routine annual exams or wellness check-ups at least four to six weeks in advance. This helps ensure you get your preferred date, time, and provider.

Q: Can I schedule appointments for multiple family members on the same day?
A: Yes, our scheduling department will make every effort to book back-to-back appointments for family members. Please mention this request when you call the scheduling office.


## Billing & Insurance

Q: What health insurance plans does the hospital accept?
A: Saint Jude Hospital accepts most major commercial insurance plans, including Blue Cross Blue Shield, Aetna, Cigna, and UnitedHealthcare. We also accept Medicare and Medicaid. Please call your provider to confirm.

Q: Can I get an out-of-pocket cost estimate for a procedure?
A: Yes, you can request a cost estimate by calling our financial services desk at (555) 0192. Please have the specific procedure name and your insurance details ready when you call.

Q: What payment options are available for hospital bills?
A: You can pay your bill online through the patient portal, by mail with a check, or by calling our billing office. We accept all major credit cards, personal checks, and debit cards.

Q: Does the hospital offer financial assistance or charity care?
A: Yes, Saint Jude Hospital offers financial assistance programs for patients who are uninsured or underinsured. Eligibility is based on income and family size. Contact our financial counselors at (555) 0195.

Q: Can I set up a payment plan for my hospital bill?
A: Yes, we offer interest-free monthly payment plans to help manage your healthcare expenses. Please call the billing department at (555) 0199 to discuss options and set up a plan.

Q: Who should I contact if I have a question about my bill?
A: For any billing inquiries, please call our billing office at (555) 0199. You can also send a secure message directly to the billing team through the MyStJude patient portal.

Q: How can I request an itemized billing statement?
A: You can request an itemized statement by calling the billing office or through the MyStJude patient portal. The statement will be mailed to your address or uploaded to the portal within five business days.

Q: What is the difference between a copay, coinsurance, and a deductible?
A: A copay is a fixed fee paid at the visit. A deductible is the amount you must pay before insurance begins covering costs. Coinsurance is your percentage share of the remaining costs.

Q: Who is responsible for obtaining prior authorization for a procedure?
A: Our prior authorization department will coordinate with your doctor and insurance provider to secure approval. However, we recommend confirming authorization status with your insurance company before your procedure.

Q: What happens if I receive a bill from an out-of-network provider?
A: If you receive care from an out-of-network provider, your insurance may cover less of the cost. Please contact our billing office to discuss your options or check if you qualify for billing protections.

Q: How do I dispute a charge on my hospital bill?
A: If you believe there is an error on your bill, contact the billing office at (555) 0199. A representative will review the charges against your medical record and issue a correction if needed.

Q: Why did I receive separate bills for the hospital and the doctor?
A: Hospital bills cover the facility, equipment, and nursing staff. Physician bills cover the professional services of the doctors who treated you. These are billed separately because they are separate entities.


## Departments & Services

Q: Where are the main departments located in the hospital?
A: Cardiology and Radiology are on the first floor. Orthopedics and Neurology are located on the second floor. Oncology and General Medicine are situated on the third floor of the main building.

Q: What services are offered by the Cardiology Department?
A: The Cardiology Department provides comprehensive heart care, including diagnostic testing, EKG tests, cardiac rehabilitation, and consultative services. We specialize in managing coronary artery disease and heart failure.

Q: What services are available in the Radiology Department?
A: The Radiology Department offers diagnostic imaging, including X-rays, MRI scans, CT scans, and ultrasounds. We also perform mammograms and bone density scans by appointment.

Q: What conditions does the Orthopedics Department treat?
A: The Orthopedics Department specializes in joint replacements, sports injuries, fractures, and spine care. We provide both surgical and non-surgical treatment options for bone and joint conditions.

Q: What services does General Medicine cover?
A: General Medicine provides primary care, annual physical exams, chronic disease management, and preventative health screenings. We serve as the main point of contact for your ongoing medical needs.

Q: What services are provided by the Pediatrics Department?
A: The Pediatrics Department offers well-child visits, immunizations, developmental screenings, and treatment for acute illnesses in children. We care for infants, children, and adolescents up to age eighteen.

Q: What conditions are treated by the Neurology Department?
A: The Neurology Department evaluates and treats disorders affecting the brain, spinal cord, and nerves. Common conditions managed include stroke, epilepsy, migraines, multiple sclerosis, and Parkinson's disease.

Q: What care is provided at the Oncology Center?
A: The Oncology Center provides chemotherapy, immunotherapy, radiation therapy, and support services for patients diagnosed with cancer. We offer a multidisciplinary approach tailored to each individual patient.

Q: What are the hours and location of the outpatient pharmacy?
A: The outpatient pharmacy is located in the main lobby on the first floor. It is open Monday through Friday from 8:00 AM to 9:00 PM, and weekends from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

Q: Where do I go for physical therapy services?
A: Physical therapy is located in the rehabilitation building adjacent to the main hospital. We offer physical, occupational, and speech therapy services for patients recovering from injury or surgery.

Q: Where can I get blood work or lab tests done?
A: The outpatient laboratory is located on the first floor near the main lobby. It is open Monday through Friday from 6:30 AM to 6:00 PM, and Saturdays from 8:00 AM to noon.

Q: What services does the Maternity Department provide?
A: Our Maternity Department offers prenatal care, labor and delivery suites, postpartum care, and newborn nurseries. We also host childbirth preparation and breastfeeding support classes for expectant parents.


## Patient Rights & Policies

Q: How does the hospital protect my medical privacy?
A: Saint Jude Hospital strictly complies with HIPAA regulations to protect your medical information. We do not disclose your health records or personal details without your written authorization, except as allowed by law.

Q: How can I request copies of my medical records?
A: You can request medical records by completing an Authorization for Release of Information form. These forms are available on our website or by contacting the medical records department at (555) 0150.

Q: Can I access my medical records online?
A: Yes, you can view your lab results, doctor's notes, immunizations, and upcoming appointments online. Simply log in to the MyStJude patient portal from any computer or mobile device.

Q: Can I request a second medical opinion from a hospital doctor?
A: Yes, you have the right to request a second opinion from any of our specialists. Please coordinate with your primary care physician to secure a referral and arrange an appointment.

Q: What is an advance directive or living will?
A: An advance directive is a legal document that outlines your preferences for medical treatment if you become unable to make decisions. You can submit a copy to our admissions desk to place in your chart.

Q: What is the hospital policy on photography and video recording?
A: To protect patient privacy and confidentiality, photography and video recording are strictly prohibited in patient care areas. You may take photos in public lobbies with the consent of those present.

Q: Who should I contact if I have a complaint about my care?
A: If you have concerns about your care, please contact our Patient Advocate office at (555) 0160. They will investigate your complaint and work to resolve the issue promptly.

Q: Does the hospital provide translation or sign language interpreters?
A: Yes, Saint Jude Hospital offers free translation and sign language interpreter services for all patients. Please inform the registration desk or your nurse if you require interpreter assistance.

Q: Are service animals allowed in the hospital?
A: Service dogs trained to perform specific tasks for individuals with disabilities are welcome in most public areas. Service animals must be harnessed and under control at all times. Pets are not allowed.

Q: What is the hospital smoking policy?
A: Saint Jude Hospital is a smoke-free and tobacco-free campus. Smoking, including the use of electronic cigarettes, is strictly prohibited in all buildings and on the surrounding outdoor grounds.


## Emergency & Urgent Care

Q: Where is the Emergency Department located, and what are its hours?
A: The Emergency Department is located on the East wing of the main campus off Wellness Boulevard. It is open twenty-four hours a day, seven days a week, three hundred sixty-five days a year.

Q: When should I go to the Emergency Room instead of Urgent Care?
A: Go to the Emergency Room for life-threatening symptoms like chest pain, difficulty breathing, severe bleeding, or stroke signs. Use Urgent Care for minor injuries, sprains, mild fevers, and sore throats.

Q: What are the hours of the Urgent Care clinic?
A: The Saint Jude Urgent Care clinic is open daily from 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM. No appointment is necessary, and patients are seen on a walk-in basis. It is located in the outpatient pavilion.

Q: How does the Emergency Room determine wait times?
A: Emergency patients are seen based on the severity of their condition, not the order of arrival. Patients with critical or life-threatening issues are always treated first, which can cause wait times for others.

Q: What should I bring with me to the Emergency Room?
A: If possible, bring a photo ID, your insurance card, and a list of your current medications. However, do not delay seeking emergency care to collect these items if you are experiencing a medical emergency.

Q: Can I receive emergency medical care if I do not have insurance?
A: Yes, federal law requires us to evaluate and stabilize any patient who presents with an emergency medical condition, regardless of ability to pay or insurance status. Your health is our priority.

Q: When should I call an ambulance instead of driving to the ER?
A: Call an ambulance immediately if you are experiencing severe chest pain, shortness of breath, sudden numbness, or if transport in a personal vehicle could cause harm or delay critical care.

Q: Who do I contact for medical questions after clinic hours?
A: For non-emergency questions after hours, you can call our nurse advice line at (555) 0102. A registered nurse is available to help evaluate your symptoms and advise on care options.

Q: What is the difference between an observation stay and an inpatient admission?
A: An observation stay is an outpatient service where you are monitored to determine if you need to be admitted. An inpatient admission means your doctor has formally admitted you to the hospital for ongoing care.

Q: Where do I go if I am experiencing a mental health emergency?
A: If you are experiencing a psychiatric crisis, you can go to our Emergency Department, call the national crisis line at 988, or call our emergency psychiatric services at (555) 0180.

Q: Does the hospital have pediatric emergency care?
A: Yes, our Emergency Department includes a dedicated pediatric section staffed by specialists trained in child emergency medicine. We provide emergency care for children and infants of all ages.

Q: What should I do if someone is exposed to a poison or toxic substance?
A: Call the National Poison Control Center immediately at (800) 222-1222 for guidance. If the person is unconscious, having a seizure, or struggling to breathe, call 911 or go to the nearest ER.

Q: Does the Emergency Room remain open during severe weather?
A: Yes, our Emergency Department is an essential service and remains open twenty-four seven during severe weather, natural disasters, and power outages. We are equipped with emergency backup systems.

Q: Where can I get follow-up care after an Emergency Room visit?
A: We recommend scheduling a follow-up appointment with your primary care provider within three to five days of an ER visit. If you need help finding a doctor, call our referral line at (555) 0103.
